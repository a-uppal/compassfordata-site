Why teams need this

Most AI programs fail upstream — in the data layer

Enterprises spend heavily on models, platforms, and pilots before they know whether the underlying data is usable, trustworthy, or fit for the specific decision in front of them. DATA Compass closes that gap.

01

You don't know what "ready" means

Teams use different definitions of quality, FAIR maturity, and governance readiness. That creates debate instead of action.

02

Scores are often not defensible

Manual checklists and black-box assessments are hard to trust, hard to reproduce, and hard to take into leadership or audit conversations.

03

Remediation is usually too vague

Even when tools identify issues, they rarely tell you what to fix first, who should own it, and what business value the fix unlocks.

How it works

A clear three-step workflow

The page should make the evaluation model feel fast and understandable. This version focuses on a simple, sales-friendly motion.

1

Connect the dataset

Upload a file or connect to the source system directly. The goal is quick visibility with minimal operational friction.

  • CSV, Excel, JSON, Parquet, and structured enterprise sources
  • Connect in place where possible
  • No forced platform migration
2

Run the assessment

Compass evaluates quality, FAIR maturity, governance, and AI fitness using deterministic checks tied to evidence.

  • Readiness scoring across multiple dimensions
  • Evidence-linked findings and provenance
  • Designed for regulated decision-making
3

Act on the result

Instead of a static report, teams get a prioritized list of actions, ownership guidance, and practical next steps.

  • Highest-value issues first
  • Suggested owners and remediation path
  • Ready for governance or sprint planning

Q

Data quality and statistical health

Catch issues that make downstream analytics and AI unreliable before they become expensive.

  • Completeness, validity, consistency, and structural profiling
  • Distribution anomalies, outliers, and label issues
  • Signals relevant to ML fitness and data stability
F

FAIR and semantic readiness

Move beyond vague FAIR claims to a more explicit view of findability, interoperability, and reuse.

  • FAIR-aligned assessment and semantic signals
  • Controlled vocabulary and ontology-aware checks
  • Structured interoperability framing for enterprise teams
G

Governance and regulated use

Support environments where provenance, ownership, privacy, and controls are not optional.

  • Provenance and evidence linkage
  • Privacy and policy-oriented checks
  • Designed for regulated and audit-conscious workflows
A

AI fitness and actionability

Translate data condition into a practical answer for AI and analytics programs.

  • Assess readiness in context, not in isolation
  • Link findings to remediation priorities
  • Help teams decide whether to proceed, fix, or defer
